Run With Us


Platform:  iOS,Android | Time: 15 weeks |  Role: Programmer

Run with us was developed as a full semester project in collaboration with the USA Track and Field Foundation. Our clients wanted a game that they could use in schools to promote Track and Field activity. As the main motive of the game was to promote physical activity, we designed the game around actual physical movements. Players move through the levels by actually running and jumping. Check out the video below, it gives an overall idea of the game.


Features


The game has three main features -


In Game Levels

The game has 12 in game levels and every level rewards the player with experience points, through which players can unlock new building components for the level builder.


Level Builder

Level builder is a building tool that the players can use to build custom tracks and challenge their friends in order to gain experience points. The level builder was designed with multiple features in order to make level building for players easy and fast. It has features like snap, overlap detection, auto scroll etc.


On Line Challege Mode

The game contains Facebook friend integration. Players can play custom tracks built by their friends and also challege them with their own levels. From playtests we found that this feature keep players engaged and also brings out their creative side in building levels of different kinds through the level builder. There also exists an additional feature called the live event that our clients will use in schools to conduct asynchronous multiplayer competitive games for larger groups. In live events, players need to play 3 levels and the first three to finish is rewarded.

Muse Meow


Platform:  PS Move | Time: 3 weeks |  Roles: Producer, Programmer, Game Designer

Muse Meow was built as a part of the story round in Building Virtual World course. The game is about a cat who tries to flee from a dog and the guest has to help the cat by moving the paintings in the scene. As the story is about a cat, all the paintings were redrawn into cat paintings as shown below.


Challenges
While playtesting we found that the guests did not understand how they can help the cat. They couldn't make out if they needed to move or rotate the paintings. There was a huge disconnect between what interactions the game wanted out of its players and what the players expected the interactions to be.

We decided to provide hints to help players derive the interactions they had to perform. As the game was intended for all kinds of audience we found out that some players easily understood the hints whereas some couldn't understand it at all. Therefore we planned to design an approach where each painting would start with indirect hints and depending on the player's performance it would gradually start to provide more direct hints. As all the paintings had different kind of interactions it became almost impossible to programatically give the correct hints at the right time. Therefore, we decided to compose a song and designed the lyrics in such a way that the right hints can be given based on player's performance. If the player is performing well more obscured hints will be given and if not the players will be given easier hints.

Initially we thought of recording different sections of the song separately and then programatically changing the lyrics but during playtesting we discovered that live music made the players enjoy the game more. Therefore we decided to have live music in this game.

Stomp Stomp Smash


Platform: Microsoft Kinect| Time: 2 weeks |  Roles: Programmer

Stomp stomp smash was built as part of the indirect control round in Building Virtual World course. Indirect control is a theory in game design where the guest is given the freedom to do whatever he wants but the game design indirectly draws the guest towards performing the required objectives of the game. In this round, we were told not to provide any instructions in the game. Therefore, we had to rely on instincts and visual concepts to guide the guest to do the actions we want.



The game is about a monster whose baby is taken away by the army. The guest has to play as the monster and traverse through the city to save her baby. We used Microsoft Kinect to detect guest movements and the guest has to keep stomping at the same place to move forward. When we play tested the game we found out that naive guests did everything other than stomping. Therefore in order to make them stomp we brought in tanks near their feet and made the tanks fire at the monster. This made the guest to stomp on the enenmy tanks that helped them to learn how to move forward. Being an open world environment, the other issue that we faced was that the guests were not going towards the end tower where the baby was kept. Therefore, in order to indirectly control the guests we kept the helicopter with the baby in the field of view so that they know where they should be heading.


Holy Sheep


Platform: PS Move + Oculus Rift| Time: 2 weeks |  Roles: Programmer

Holy Sheep game was built as an assignment for the Building Virtual World course in the Entertainment Technology Center. The objective of the assignment was to build a virtual world where there are two characters A and B, in which character B is after character A and the guest has to help character A by performing some action.



In the Holy Sheep game Character A is a sheep in a ship who is being attacked by Character B that is a kraken. The kraken is trying to capsize the ship by drilling holes into the ship. The role of the guest is to block the holes in the ship by using fishes in the sea. The guest is a squid which can capture fishes using its suction cup tentacles and then shoot the fishes to the hole's in the ship to block it. The guest needs to keep the ship afloat till it reaches land


Indian Jonas


Platform: Kinect 2, Phidgets, Leap Motion, Makey Makey| Time: 3 weeks |  Roles: Programmer

Indian Jonas is a game which was built as part of the Building Virtual World course at the Entertainment Technology Center. The objective of the game is similar to Indiana Jones where the player encounters a series of hurdles to retrieve the Holy Grail. The game is an amalgamation of real and virtual puzzles, where real puzzles have direct impact on the virtual world.

For instance, the player needs to walk through a narrow path consisting of boobie traps. These traps trigger arrows in the virtual world which the player need to avoid by dodging.

I fulfilled the programmer role in the project, where I was responsible for integrating multiple platforms to develop the experience. The platforms included Kinect 2, Leap Motion, Makey Makey and DMX Lights.
