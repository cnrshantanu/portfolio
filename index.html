Chromium Engine Optimisation Project

This project was based on the Chromium Engine. We were a group of 7 in India and concentrated on the optimisation and customisation of webkit engine and chromium graphics engine. From the team two of us were responsible for optimising the chromium graphics engine so that it worked better on Sony devices. My role was to investigate areas that could be optimised by collecting profiling data like latency, paint time and then come up with new design approaches for the optimisation. This project gave me indepth knowledge of the chromium engine and also helped me understand the importance of design and coding standards. In addition, as Chromium source is very huge the project helped me a lot in developing an approach to debug and find bugs faster in the source. Due to confidentiality reasons, I cannot go deep into the optimisations implemented.

Muse Meow


Muse Meow was built as part of the story round in Building Virtual World course. The game is about a cat who is trying to flee from a dog and the guest has to help the cat by moving the paintings. As the story is about a cat, we decided to change all the famous paintings and turn them into cat paintings as shown below.


Challenges
While playtesting we found that the guests were not able to understand on how can they help the cat, like do they need to move rotate and we found that there was a disconnect between what the guests wanted to do and what he actually needed to do.

We didn't want to explicitly give hints but instead wanted to start from hints that are hard to understand to clear cut hints. As all the painting had different kind of interactions it became almost impossible to programatically give the correct hints at the right time. Therefore, we decided to compose a song and designed the lyrics in such a way that we give hints on how the player is performing. If the player is performing well we will give more obscured hints in the next painting or we would give easier hints.

At first we thought of recording seperate part of the songs and then programatically changing it but during playtesting we found out that live music made the guest enjoy the game more. Therefore we decided to go with live music.



Stomp Stomp Smash


Stomp stomp smash was built as part of the indirect control round in Building Virtual World course. Indirect control is a theory in game design where you give the guest freedom but design your world such that they are drawn towards performing the objectives of the game. In this round, our games were played by naive guests and we were told not to provide any instructions in the game. Therefore, we had to rely on instincts and visual concepts to guide the guest to do the actions we want.

The game is about a monster whose baby is taken away by the army. The guest has to play as the monster and traverse through the city to save her baby. We used Microsoft Kinect to detect guest movements. The guest has to keep stomping at the same place to move forward. When we play tested the game we found out that naive guests did everything other than stomping. Therefore in order to make them stomp we brought in tanks near their feet and made the tanks fire at the monster. This made the guest to stomp on the enenmy tanks and helped them to learn how to move forward. Being an open world environment, the other issue that we faced was that the guest was not going towards the end tower where the baby was kept. Therefore, in order to indirectly control the guest we kept the helicopter baby in the field of view so that they know where they should be heading.
